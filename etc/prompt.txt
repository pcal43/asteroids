This project contains the skeleton for a pico-8 game.  It includes
a placeholder for a top-level controller class as well as a title screen
and a game screen.  It estsablishes the conventions I'd like to follow
for object-oriented style programming in lua of coarse-grained objects
(such as screens).


I'd like to build on this skeleton by developing a game like the old
Atari Asertoids arcade game.  I'd like all of the graphics to be drawn 
geometrically with the line and circle functions.

The player controls a spaceship that is represented by an isocoles 
triangle.  The 'nose' of the triangle should be a 30 degree angle.
When the player presses the left or right button, the ship rotates 5 degrees
to the left or right.  When the player pushes the x button, this is applies
thrust to the ship - it gets forward momentum at the rate of 5px per second
and which decays at the rate of 1px per second if the thrust button is
released. 

Pressing the O button causes the player's ship to fire small 
projectiles in a straight line from the nose of the ship which travel for 64
pixels at the rate of 10 pixels per second.  After that, they disappear from play.

The game should randomly materialize asteroids across the playfield 
which move along random vectors at speeds ranging from 1 to 5px per second.
There are three sizes of asteroids, large medium and small.  Asteroids should
be drawn as circles using the circle funcion.  A large asteroid is 16px in diamter, 
a medium is 12 and a small is 8.

If the player's shot collides with an asteroid, it is replaced by two asteroids
of the next smaller size, moving again on a random vector.  If a small asteroid
is hit by a shot, it is removed from play.

If the player's ship collides with an asteroid, it is destroyed and the game
returns to the title screen.

The player receives points for destroying asteroids - 10 for a large, 20 for a medium
and 30 for a small.  The score is displayed in the upper right corner of the screen.


All of the game logic should be implemented in the update and draw functions
of GameScreen.lua.
