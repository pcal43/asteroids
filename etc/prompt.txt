This project contains a skeleton for a PICO-8 game. It includes a top-level controller, a title screen, and a game screen. The project establishes conventions for object-oriented-style Lua using coarse-grained objects such as screens with `update()` and `draw()` methods.

Extend this skeleton to implement a game inspired by Atari Asteroids using purely geometric rendering. All graphics must be drawn using `line()` and `circ()`/`circfill()`. Do not use sprites.

All gameplay logic should live in `GameScreen.lua`. Follow the object-oriented conventions already present. Define objects such as `Ship`, `Asteroid`, `Bullet`, and `UFO` as Lua tables with methods. Each object should implement `update(dt)` and `draw()`. Maintain collections for asteroids, bullets, enemy bullets, and UFOs. Use a consistent delta time system in seconds for all movement and timing.

The player controls a spaceship represented by an isosceles triangle. This requirement is important: the ship must be visibly triangular, not a narrow wedge or nearly straight line. The triangle’s nose angle must be 30 degrees total, meaning 15 degrees on each side of the forward axis. The ship should be about two-thirds the size it was previously; specifically, use a height of 8 pixels measured from the nose to the midpoint of the base, and compute the base width from that height and the 30 degree nose angle so the shape is correct. Draw the ship in white using `line()`.

The ship starts at the center of the screen `(64,64)`, facing upward, with zero velocity.

Controls: pressing Right rotates the ship clockwise, and pressing Left rotates the ship counterclockwise. This mapping is important and must not be reversed. Rotation should continue while the button is held, at 180 degrees per second. Pressing the X button applies thrust in the ship’s facing direction. Make thrust twice as strong as before: use acceleration of 20 pixels per second squared. When not thrusting, apply velocity damping at 1 pixel per second squared until velocity reaches zero. Cap the maximum ship velocity at 20 pixels per second. While thrusting, draw a red equilateral triangle behind the ship, pointing opposite the ship’s facing direction.

Pressing the O button fires a bullet from the ship’s nose in the current facing direction. Bullets travel at 20 pixels per second and disappear after traveling 64 pixels. Bullets are 2 pixels in size and orange in color. Add a small firing cooldown of about 0.2 seconds.

At the start of the game, spawn 10 large asteroids at random positions, ensuring none are within 20 pixels of the player. Asteroids move in random directions at speeds between 5 and 10 pixels per second.

There are three asteroid sizes: large with 16 pixel diameter, medium with 12 pixel diameter, and small with 8 pixel diameter. Asteroids are drawn as outlined circles using `circ()`. When a large asteroid is hit, it splits into two medium asteroids. When a medium asteroid is hit, it splits into two small asteroids. Small asteroids are destroyed when hit. New asteroids inherit the parent position and move in random directions.

Use simple circular collision detection for all interactions. Player bullets destroy asteroids. The player ship is destroyed on collision with an asteroid. Player bullets destroy UFOs.

When the player’s ship is destroyed, do not immediately respawn it. Instead, play a short destruction animation at the ship’s position, then remove the ship from play entirely for a short delay of at least 3 seconds before spawning the next life. During this time the player cannot move or fire. When placing the next ship, care must be taken to ensure the spawn location is not within 30 pixels of any asteroid. If the center of the screen is unsafe, wait until a suitable spawn location becomes available rather than spawning the ship into danger. When the new ship is finally placed, reset its velocity to zero and grant temporary invulnerability of about 2 seconds.

The game world wraps on all edges with toroidal topology. If an object moves past one edge of the 128 by 128 screen, it should appear on the opposite side. This applies to the ship, asteroids, bullets, and UFOs.

The player starts with 3 lives. Display remaining lives as small upward-pointing isosceles triangles in the upper-left corner. These life icons should match the player ship shape and proportions, scaled down appropriately.

The player earns points: 10 for large asteroids, 20 for medium asteroids, 30 for small asteroids, and 100 for destroying a UFO. Display the score in the upper-right corner.

When all asteroids are destroyed, wait 3 seconds, then spawn a new wave of 10 large asteroids following the same rules.

At random intervals between 30 and 60 seconds, spawn a UFO. It travels horizontally across the screen from left to right or right to left at about 15 pixels per second. The UFO should be drawn as a green horizontal rectangle, roughly comparable in overall size to the player’s ship. It periodically fires 2 pixel green bullets in random directions, about once every 1 to 2 seconds. UFO bullets can destroy both the player and asteroids, but asteroid destruction by UFO bullets does not award points. If the player’s bullet hits the UFO, it is destroyed and awards 100 points. If the UFO collides with the player, both are destroyed.

Ensure clean separation of update logic and rendering logic. The final implementation should be complete, readable, and idiomatic PICO-8 Lua, with no placeholder code.
