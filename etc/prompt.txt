This project contains the skeleton for a pico-8 game.  It includes
a placeholder for a top-level controller class as well as a title screen
and a game screen.  It estsablishes the conventions I'd like to follow
for object-oriented style programming in lua of coarse-grained objects
(such as screens).

I'd like to build on this skeleton by developing a game like the old
Atari Asertoids arcade game.  I'd like all of the graphics to be drawn 
geometrically with the line and circle functions.

The player controls a spaceship that is represented by an isosceles 
triangle.  The 'nose' of the triangle should be a 30 degree angle.  The 
attitude of the triangle should be 12px.  The lines should be drawn in white.

When the player presses the right button, the ship rotates by 15 degrees 
clockwise; pressing left rotates counterclockwise.  The ship should contine
to rotate for as long as the button is held.  At the start of the game, the
ship is drawn in the center of the screen with it's nose pointing up.

When the player pushes the x button, this is applies thrust to the ship - 
it gets forward momentum at the rate of 10px per second and which decays 
at the rate of 1px per second if the thrust button is released.  While the
ship's thrust is active, a red equilateral triangle should be drawn at the base
of the player's ship, pointing in the opposite direction as the nose of the ship.

Pressing the O button causes the player's ship to fire small 2px
projectiles in a straight line from the nose of the ship which travel for 64
pixels at the rate of 20 pixels per second.  After that, they disappear 
from play.  The projectiles should be orange in color.

The game should randomly materialize 10 large asteroids across the playfield 
which move along random vectors at speeds ranging from 5 to 10px per second.
Care should be taken to ensure that none of the initial asteroid positions are within
20px of the player's ship locaiton.

There are three sizes of asteroids, large medium and small.  Asteroids should
be drawn as circles using the circle funcion.  A large asteroid is 16px in diamter, 
a medium is 12 and a small is 8.

If the player's shot collides with an asteroid, it is replaced by two asteroids
of the next smaller size, moving again on a random vector.  If a small asteroid
is hit by a shot, it is removed from play.

If the player manages to destroy all of the asteroids on the playfield, there is a 
3 second pause, at which point 10 new large asteroids are placed on the screen following
the game setup rules.

If the player's ship collides with an asteroid, it is destroyed.  The 
player is given a total of three ships at the start of the game.  The number
of ships remaining in reserve is indicated with small 8px upward pointing
isosceles triangles in the upper left corner of the screen.  If three ships
are destroyed, the game returns to the title screen.

The player receives points for destroying asteroids - 10 for a large, 20 for a medium
and 30 for a small.  The score is displayed in the upper right corner of the screen.

At random, every 30 to 60 seconds, a UFO will appear.  It will move from left to
right or right to left across the screen, periodically firing 2px green projectiles
in random directions.  These projectiles can damage or asteroids the same as
the player's (though the player received no points for this).  If a projectile
hits the player's ship, the player's ship is destroyed.  If the player's projectiles
hit the UFO, it is destroyed and the player is awarded 100 points.  If the UFO and
the player collide, they are both destroyed.

The entire gamescreen is a torus: all objects, including asteroids, the player's ship,
shots and UFOs should 'warp' across the edges of the screen and appear on the other side.

All of the game logic should be implemented in the update and draw functions
of GameScreen.lua.
